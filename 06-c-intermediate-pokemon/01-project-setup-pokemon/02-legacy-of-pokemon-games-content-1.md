It all began in the late 1980s, when a Japanese game designer named **Satoshi Tajiri** envisioned a world where players could collect, trade, and battle creatures.



Tajiri’s inspiration came from his childhood love of collecting insects, and this passion drove him to create something truly unique.



![c++](https://outscal-assets.s3.ap-south-1.amazonaws.com/production/LMS/634ff2a63bfd3eecf3cfe038/09_09_2024__06_59_43.png)



He wanted to capture the joy and excitement of discovering new creatures, and his vision culminated in the creation of Pokémon.

However, bringing this idea to life was far from easy.



Tajiri founded the game development company Game Freak.

When the team at Game Freak began developing Pokémon, it was a project unlike anything else at the time.

With the help of Nintendo and Shigeru Miyamoto, the legendary creator of Mario and Zelda, Tajiri’s dream slowly became a reality.



In the year 1996, The first games, Pokémon Red and Green, were released in 1996 for the Game Boy.

These game changed the whole scenario of the gaming industry at that time.

# Evolution


---



Throughout its history, Pokémon has influenced not only the gaming industry but also popular culture. 

The games inspired a long-running anime series, which introduced characters like Ash Ketchum and Pikachu to the world. 



![c++](https://outscal-assets.s3.ap-south-1.amazonaws.com/production/LMS/634ff2a63bfd3eecf3cfe038/09_09_2024__07_28_49.png)



The Pokémon Trading Card Game became a global sensation, further expanding the franchise's reach. 

Pokémon became a household name, with a presence in everything from movies to merchandise.

> The Pokémon franchise has earned an estimated total revenue of **over $100 billion**, making it the highest-grossing media franchise of all time.



Since its inception in 1996, Pokémon has generated billions across various platforms, including video games, trading cards, movies, merchandise, and mobile apps like *Pokémon Go*. 



Video games have consistently driven the brand's financial success, with over 390 million units sold across multiple platforms.
