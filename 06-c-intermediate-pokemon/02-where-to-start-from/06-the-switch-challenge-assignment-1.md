**Your Task:** The great Professor Oak is waiting for you to finalize your first Pokémon selection. But this time, you will wield the **Switch Statement** to make it even better!



        🟢 **Step 1: The Player's Choice**

"Write a `switch` statement to handle the player’s choice of Pokémon. Each case should correspond to a Pokémon."



        🟢 **Step 2: Add a Surprise**

"If the player makes an invalid choice, don’t end the game—let Professor Oak surprise them with Pikachu! (Even the professor likes to have some fun!)"




---



**Here’s what Professor Oak expects you to do:**



1. **Use a **`**switch**`** statement** instead of if-else to handle the player’s choice.
2. For **Choice 1**, assign **Charmander** to the player.
3. For **Choice 2**, assign **Bulbasaur** to the player.
4. For **Choice 3**, assign **Squirtle** to the player.
5. For any other choice, **surprise the player** with **Pikachu**!
6. Don’t forget to include **fun dialogue** for each choice! Make Professor Oak's words memorable!




---



**Example Output:**



- `**Player chooses 1:"Professor Oak: A fiery choice! Charmander is yours!"**`



- `**Player chooses 2:"Professor Oak: A fine choice! Bulbasaur is always ready to grow on you!"**`



- `**Player chooses 3:"Professor Oak: Splendid! Squirtle will keep you cool under pressure!"**`



- `**Player chooses an invalid option: "Professor Oak: Hmm, that doesn't seem right. Let me choose for you… Just kidding! Let's go with Pikachu, the surprise guest!"**`
