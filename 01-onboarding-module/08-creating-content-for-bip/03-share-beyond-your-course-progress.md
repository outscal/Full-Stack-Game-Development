The content of your post doesn’t even have to be limited to your progress in the course. Share more about your experiences and journey.



For example:

- If you were impressed by any game mechanic in a game you have played, you can make a post about it asking people “This is how I think the “**Size Change”** mechanic in the Mario game might have been implemented, what do you guys think? Is there a better way to do it? Let me know in the comments.”
- If you looked at some new gameplay or trailer of an upcoming game and were impressed by it, you can share that as well in a LinkedIn post explaining what you found so good about this upcoming game.
- If you went to a game dev conference like IGDC or have been part of a game jam, you can share about that experience as well, while sharing some photos of the event, like in this example [here](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/developerswork_igdc2023-gamedevelopment-unity-activity-7126432816950558720-NTq0?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop).
- If you made some really good friends from the Outscal Community, maybe someone from your clanmates, who is on this journey with you, you could give them a shoutout as well and tell us how much that person has helped you, community matters.



There are numerous examples, your creativity is the only limit. Basically, as long as you are creating posts around the gaming industry and **YOU** its fine.
