The journey to become a game developer is filled with ups and downs. We’ve all been there—struggling with a concept we just can’t grasp, getting rejected after what seemed like a great interview, or having our code get torn apart during review. But that’s all part of the process.



![](https://tenor.com/view/winnie-the-pooh-up-and-gif-19453028.gif)



Everybody’s journey has ups and downs, and yours is going to be a similar one as well. We want you to know that it’s okay to share the challenges along with the wins.**  In fact, we encourage it!**



We always tell students to think of LinkedIn as a “Professional Instagram”. But like Instagram, you don’t need to showcase only your good sides, you can even post about things like:

- An assignment that you are struggling to complete
- A concept that you are finding challenging to implement or understand
- Sharing your experience of a job interview that you were not able to crack
- A rejection you got in code review
- Simply about not being able to perform due to some reasons



**Share your struggles, too**. Ask questions. Start discussions. The community is here to help.



Most people on LinkedIn (especially our Outscal community) love to help people whenever they get a chance to, making it the perfect place to share and discuss what you are working on unapologetically.



The people who care most about helping you succeed will offer guidance and support. You will find mentors, peers, collaborators, and they will all become a part of your journey too.



Whether your post is about some achievements or challenges, it doesn’t matter, what matters is that **the post is about YOU and YOU are what matters the most.**



Cause its your journey and you are the main character of this story.
