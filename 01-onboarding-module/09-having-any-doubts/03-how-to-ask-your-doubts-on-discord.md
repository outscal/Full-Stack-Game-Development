In the last section we told you that most people don't know how to ask the correct questions.



If the doubts are not asked or framed correctly, then doubt solvers are going to have a tough time solving your problems.



If you have ever checked out `**stackoverflow**`. The questions you will find there are always very well framed and easily understandable.



You need to help doubt solvers a little by following the below guidelines so that can help you as well:

- Make sure you have written what you want to convey clearly
- The details given in the questions and the way it is frame has to be very precise and easily understandable.
- All the context regarding the doubt must be given by you. Like code screenshots, gameplay videos, etc.
- Don't give half information. Half information will always create confusion. Provide any kind of context associated with your doubts.
- Always use the relevant **helper-roles** to notify others about your doubts.



What are **helper-roles**? We'll see in the next section.
