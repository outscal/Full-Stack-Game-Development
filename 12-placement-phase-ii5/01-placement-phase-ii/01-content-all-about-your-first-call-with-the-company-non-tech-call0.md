<ul>
<li>Mostly the first interview will be a pure non-technical interview.</li>
<li>DO NOT say expected CTC ever in the first call or also in the last negotiation call.</li>
<li>Be honest and represent yourself as a human.</li>
<li>If there is any negative question like “tell me something about your weaknesses”
<ol>
<li>Then come up with smart weaknesses like being so harsh on myself. Even though there is a genuine reason for something not working I never give myself comfort.</li>
<li>Always end such questions with “<strong>but” → Positive ending</strong> by saying I am working hard on a regular basis to overcome this.</li>
</ol>
</li>
<li>Never bitch about your ex. company/co-workers, this will scratch you out straight away from the interview.</li>
<li>If any company asks you about Outscal or asks how much Outscal pays you
<ol>
<li>Represent Outscal as an upskilling platform, and tell the organization that you improving your skillset in Outscal under the guidance of professionally experienced mentors of Unity and Data structures.</li>
</ol>
</li>
<li>Tricky questions (Refer to the video for answers) -
<ol>
<li>Tell me three reasons why we should not hire you.</li>
<li>Can you tell me some negative points about your previous company?</li>
<li>What did you dislike about your last job?</li>
<li>Why should we hire you?</li>
<li>Why did you leave your last job?</li>
<li>Why are you changing/shifting the job?</li>
<li>Tell me something about your weaknesses.</li>
</ol>
</li>
</ul>
