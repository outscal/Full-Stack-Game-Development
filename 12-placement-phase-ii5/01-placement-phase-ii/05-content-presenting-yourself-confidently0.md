<p><strong>[Video Discussions]</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Introduce yourself will be the most important question and this will be a deal maker or deal-breaker for you.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>This is most underrated but VVIMP question that everyone fucks up.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Your answer can differ as per the interview rounds</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Case: 1 → Non-tech round / You don't know which round is this / final CTO/CEO round / Multiple interviewers are in front of you</p>
<ol>
<li>Always cap your answer within 3 mins MAX.</li>
<li>Here you need to give your overall introduction.
<ol>
<li>Start with the basic personal introduction with your name, from where you are and what is your education.</li>
<li>The second point must cover your passion for your profession and <strong>skills.</strong> You can talk about technical experience / technical projects. This point should take 50% weightage of the answer.</li>
<li>End your introduction with some of your hobbies and how you are as a person.</li>
</ol>
</li>
</ol>
</li>
<li>
<p>Case: 2 → Technical Round (2 mins MAX)</p>
<ol>
<li>Here you should not include any non-technical points about yourself.</li>
<li>Start with a basic intro, name, location and education and directly jump to your skills and technical expertise.</li>
<li>Be precise and careful while sharing any technical skills here bcoz your interview can take shape as per this answer.</li>
<li>Introduce yourself as an engineer who wants to solve problems in the best possible and most efficient way.</li>
<li>You don't need to share any non-tech things at the end or hobbies, just keep it super short but sexy, tech people don't like bullshit.</li>
</ol>
</li>
</ul>
